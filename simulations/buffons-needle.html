Buffon's Needle Simulation

This interactive demo recreates Buffon's Needle: a classic probabilistic experiment that uses simple geometry to estimate ùúã. By randomly dropping needles onto a lined surface and tracking how often they cross a line, we approximate ùúã using geometric probability.

Here, we explore the idea with Monte Carlo sampling where each needle drop is a trial in a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) process, allowing for scalable, adaptive estimation. As the number of trials increases, the approximation converges toward ùúã.
